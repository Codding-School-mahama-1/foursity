Vaccine Information

Learn about the vaccines available in our program

Pentavalent Vaccine

Protects against five deadly diseases: Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis, Hepatitis B, and Haemophilus influenzae type B.

Dosage Schedule:

  • First dose: 6 weeks
  • Second dose: 10 weeks
  • Third dose: 14 weeks

Pneumococcal Vaccine (PCV)

Protects against pneumonia, meningitis, and other infections caused by Streptococcus pneumoniae bacteria.

Dosage Schedule:

  • First dose: 6 weeks
  • Second dose: 10 weeks
  • Third dose: 14 weeks

Oral Polio Vaccine (OPV)

Protects against poliomyelitis, a highly infectious viral disease that can lead to permanent paralysis.

Dosage Schedule:

  • At birth
  • 6 weeks
  • 10 weeks
  • 14 weeks

Measles Vaccine

Protects against measles, a highly contagious viral disease that can cause severe complications, especially in children.

Dosage Schedule:

  • First dose: 9 months
  • Second dose: 15-18 months

COVID-19 Vaccine

Protects against COVID-19, reducing severe illness, hospitalization, and death from the virus.

Available Vaccines:

  • Pfizer-BioNTech
  • Moderna
  • Johnson & Johnson
  • AstraZeneca

Cholera Vaccine

Protects against cholera, a waterborne disease that can cause severe diarrhea and dehydration.

Dosage Schedule:

  • Two doses, 2 weeks apart
  • Protection lasts about 3 years

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about vaccination in refugee settings

Yes, vaccines are thoroughly tested for safety before being approved for use. All vaccines provided in our program meet international safety standards and are approved by the World Health Organization. Side effects are usually mild (like a sore arm or low-grade fever) and go away within a few days.

In crowded living conditions like refugee camps, diseases can spread quickly. Vaccination protects individuals and creates community immunity, making it harder for diseases to spread. This is especially important for vulnerable groups like children, pregnant women, and the elderly.

Please bring any existing vaccination records if available. If this is your first visit, no documents are required. All residents of the camp are eligible for free vaccination regardless of documentation status.

Yes, some vaccines are especially important during pregnancy. Tetanus vaccine protects both mother and newborn, and flu vaccine is recommended. Some vaccines (like MMR) should be given before pregnancy or after delivery. Our health workers can advise you on which vaccines are safe during pregnancy.

It's never too late to catch up on missed vaccines! Our health workers can create a catch-up schedule for your child. Bring any vaccination records you have, and we'll determine which vaccines are needed.
